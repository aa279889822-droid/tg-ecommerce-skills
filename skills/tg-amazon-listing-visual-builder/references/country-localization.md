# Country Localization

## Core Rule

Localize for the buyer, not just the language. Match country, marketplace habits, units, household scenes, climate, trust expectations, and after-sales reassurance. Avoid stereotypes, flags, costumes, landmarks, or cultural symbols unless they naturally fit the product and sales context.

## Language Defaults

- United States: American English, imperial units when relevant.
- Canada: English or French depending marketplace/listing; use metric with common local context.
- United Kingdom: British English, metric with UK spelling.
- Germany: German, precise and evidence-led wording.
- France: French, lifestyle-aware but still concrete.
- Italy: Italian, warm and benefit-led.
- Spain: Spanish, clear everyday use cases.
- Japan: Japanese, detail-oriented, polite, reassurance-heavy.
- Australia: English, practical outdoor/home context when relevant.
- Vietnam: Vietnamese, value clarity, durability, usage reassurance.
- Thailand: Thai, friendly concise phrasing, confidence and ease of use.
- Malaysia: English or Malay based on audience; avoid over-assuming ethnicity or religion; use practical household context.
- Philippines: English by default for ecommerce, optionally Filipino for mass-market local tone.
- Singapore: English by default, concise, premium/practical, space-efficient living context.

When the country is multilingual and the user does not specify language, choose the most common ecommerce language for that marketplace and state the assumption.

## Local Culture Elements

Use subtle and product-relevant elements:

- Home products: local apartment/home layouts, countertop styles, room sizes, common daily scenarios.
- Outdoor products: local climate and terrain, but avoid unsafe or extreme scenes.
- Kitchen products: familiar meal-prep context without making unsupported food-safety claims.
- Beauty/personal care: local skin-tone diversity and modest claims; avoid medical promises.
- Electronics: local plug/socket/voltage only when the user confirms compatibility.
- Pet products: common household pet scenes, no unsafe animal handling.
- Baby/mother products: safety-first, calm tone, no unsupported health or developmental claims.

Do not use religious, political, ethnic, medical, or national identity symbols as decoration unless the product specifically requires them and the user confirms appropriateness.

## After-Sales Slogan Rules

The after-sales slogan must reduce purchase anxiety in the target language. It should be short, credible, and supportable. Do not promise warranties, refunds, local service, or response times unless provided by the user.

Use these safe patterns:

- Assurance: "Buy with confidence" style.
- Support: "Friendly support when you need help" style.
- Easy setup: "Clear setup support included" style when instructions are included.
- Quality check: "Carefully checked before shipping" only if true.
- Replacement/returns: mention only if the policy is provided.
- Warranty: mention only when duration and scope are provided.

If policy details are missing, use soft reassurance rather than hard claims.

## Example After-Sales Tone By Market

- US: direct, confidence-led, benefit plus support.
- Germany: precise, calm, no exaggerated emotion.
- Japan: polite,安心感, detail and support.
- Thailand: warm, simple, trust-building.
- Vietnam: practical, value and durability focused.
- Malaysia: clear, respectful, service-oriented.
- Philippines: friendly, simple, helpful.
- Singapore: concise, premium, reliable.

## Units And Text

Convert units to local expectations when dimensions or measurements appear. For visuals, avoid tiny text. Use short benefit phrases, not paragraphs. If exact measurements are missing, use placeholders and mark them as needing confirmation.
